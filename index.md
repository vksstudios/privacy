# Privacy Policy for Reader App

**Effective Date:** March 10, 2026

## 1. Introduction
Welcome to Reader ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring a secure, straightforward experience. This Privacy Policy explains how our mobile application handles your information.

Reader is purposefully designed as a robust, standalone offline document and media consumption tool. We understand how critical digital accessibility is—especially for students who may rely on mobile devices as their primary or sole tool for studying in the absence of a personal computer. With Reader, essential academic materials, ranging from lecture slides (PPTX) and lengthy reading assignments (PDF, DOCX) to programming code snippets and audio recordings, can be seamlessly accessed right from a smartphone. This makes the app an invaluable educational companion, bridging the digital divide and ensuring students can learn anywhere, anytime, without needing an internet connection. 

We built this app so educators can confidently recommend a tool that respects student privacy while delivering desktop-class file reading capabilities on mobile devices.

## 2. Information We Collect (Or Rather, What We Don't)
Reader is fundamentally built on a local-first, privacy-by-design architecture. 

**Local Data Processing:** 
All files—including PDFs, Word documents, PowerPoint presentations, Excel spreadsheets, source code files, images, and audio tracks—are processed entirely on your local device. We **do not** upload, transmit, analyze, or store your documents, personal files, or viewing habits on any external servers or cloud environments.

**App Preferences and History:**
To provide a seamless and continuous user experience, the application locally stores:
*   **Recent Files History:** A local registry of files you've recently opened, allowing quick, frictionless access to your ongoing studies or work.
*   **App Settings:** User preferences, such as your "Keep Awake" toggles, which ensure your screen doesn't unexpectedly turn off while you are studying lengthy documents.

This minimal data is strictly saved on your device's internal storage using standard system preferences mechanisms. It is never transmitted securely or insecurely to us or any third parties.

## 3. Third-Party Services
Because Reader operates independently offline and processes files locally using built-in, sandboxed rendering engines, it does not integrate with any external tracking software, telemetry frameworks, or advertising SDKs. What happens on your device stays entirely on your device.

## 4. Permissions We Request
To function correctly and provide its intended educational utility, Reader requests the following standard device permissions:
*   **Storage / File Access:** Required strictly to allow you to locate, open, and display the documents, media, and code files you choose to view within the application. 
*   **Wake Lock (Keep Awake):** Utilized to prevent your device's screen from dimming or sleeping while you are actively reading lectures, reviewing code, or presenting.

## 5. Data Security
Because your offline files or academic materials never leave your device, the security of those documents inherently relies on the security of the device itself. We strongly recommend utilizing standard operating system security measures, such as screen locks, biometrics, and device encryption, to further protect your locally stored data.

## 6. Commitment to Education
Not every student has continuous access to a traditional laptop or PC, yet modern education demands the ability to review complex file formats constantly. By transforming any mobile device into a comprehensive academic suite capable of rendering high-fidelity files safely, we aim to eliminate barriers to learning. Reader provides a distraction-free, privacy-respecting environment where students can focus entirely on their growth.

## 7. Changes to This Policy
We may periodically update this Privacy Policy to reflect app updates or regulatory requirements. Any changes will be reflected in this document alongside an updated "Effective Date." Given our commitment to an offline-first architecture, our core stance on local data processing will not change.

## 8. Contact Information
If you have any academic inquiries, feedback, or concerns regarding this Privacy Policy and how your data remains local on your device, please reach out directly to the development team.
